Website Evaluation

Sat, May 1, 2021 3-minute read

Clemson University, Industrial Engineering Department

Project Overview

The goal of this project was to evaluate Clemson University’s Industrial Engineering website. Through an in-depth analysis, we were able to detect pain points and develop solutions for improvement.

Persona Development

We developed personas in order to understand the goals and motivations of users who are most likely to interact with the Clemson Industrial Engineering Website. Prospective students and prospective faculty are two groups who are likely to seek information about an institution or program from their website. This may be the first or only interaction these individuals have with Clemson IE before coming to campus, meaning their experiences are highly valuable.

Hierarchical Task Analysis

Each task was based on the goals of each user outlined above. These represent tasks that prospective users are likely to be conducting when interacting with the website. Below is a list of tasks organized according to prospective student and faculty user groups.

Prospective Students

  • Identify course requirements
  • List what degree programs are offered (undergraduate and graduate)
  • Identify student organizations associated with IE
  • Determine how to transfer credits from another institution
  • Determine which classes are transferable to Clemson

Prospective Faculty

  • Locate job availability information
  • Determine number of current faculty
  • List the research labs within the department
  • Determine whether this program is accredited

Competitor Analysis

Figure 1. UIUC homepage contains dynamic and interactive elements. For example, the top section is a carousel that displays three items that periodically rotate and are clickable. Beneath the carousel section are recent events with an image, date, and short description of the event/article. Note the heavy emphasis on imagery, not text.

Figure 2. Georgia Tech homepage, like UIUC’s, contains a rotating carousel at the top of the page. Additionally, further down the page is a section that contains quick links for easy site navigation. These links are separated by user type (e.g., prospective student, current student, faculty, etc.).

Figure 3. UIUC - Interactive Curriculum map. For example, if you place your cursor over senior Engineering Project 2 in Senior Fall, pre- and post-requisites are automatically color coded. Clicking on course routes you to a page where you can add it to your schedule.

Figure 4. Research areas section on Georgia Tech’s website. Users are able to navigate based on their personal interests.

Figure 5. Texas A and M organizes their home page in a similar pattern to Clemson. However, hovering over a tab reveals an additional set of navigation options without leaving the home page.

Figure 6. Contact and social media information is presented cleanly and concisely.
